What Are Root Canal Treatment?

A root canal treatment is a dental procedure used to save a severely infected or damaged tooth instead of removing it. Inside every tooth is a soft tissue called the pulp, which contains nerves and blood vessels. When this pulp becomes infected due to deep decay, cracks, trauma, or repeated dental procedures, it can cause intense pain and swelling.

During a root canal treatment, the infected tissue is carefully removed, the inside of the tooth is cleaned and disinfected, and the canals are sealed to prevent future infection. The tooth is then restored with a filling or crown, allowing it to function normally again.

Unlike tooth extraction, root canal treatment preserves your natural tooth structure, maintains proper chewing ability, and prevents nearby teeth from shifting. With modern rotary endodontic technology and local anaesthesia, the procedure is comfortable, efficient, and highly successful.

Our Root Canal Treatment Process — Step by Step

1
Examination & Digital X-ray

Our dentist carefully examines your tooth and takes a digital X-ray to identify infection inside the root canals. Based on the diagnosis, a precise treatment plan is created to remove infection while preserving your natural tooth.

2
Comfortable Anaesthesia

Local anaesthesia is administered to completely numb the treatment area. You remain comfortable throughout the procedure, feeling only slight pressure but no pain.

3
Cleaning the Root Canals

Using advanced rotary endodontic instruments, the infected pulp is gently removed and the canals are cleaned, shaped, and disinfected with high precision for effective bacteria removal.

4
Sealing the Tooth

After thorough cleaning, the canals are filled with a biocompatible material and sealed to prevent future infection. This step strengthens the internal structure of the tooth.

5
Final Restoration

A durable filling or custom dental crown is placed to restore the tooth’s strength, appearance, and normal chewing function, allowing it to work just like a natural tooth again.
