What Are Dental Implants?

Living with a missing tooth affects more than your smile — it changes how you eat, speak, and feel about yourself in social situations. A dental implant is a small titanium post that is surgically placed into your jawbone, acting as an artificial tooth root. A realistic-looking crown is then attached on top, replacing the missing tooth permanently.

Unlike removable dentures, implants are fixed permanently. They require no adhesives, don't shift while eating or talking, and can last a lifetime with normal brushing and flossing. Implants also prevent jawbone loss that occurs naturally after tooth loss — preserving your facial structure for the long term.

Our Implant Process — Step by Step

1
Free Consultation & Digital Scan (Day 1)

Our implant specialist examines your jaw, takes a digital X-ray or CBCT scan, and creates a treatment plan specific to your case. You'll receive a full cost estimate with no obligation.

2
Implant Placement (45–60 mins, under local anaesthesia)

The titanium post is placed in your jawbone through a minimally invasive procedure. Most patients return to normal activity the next day. A temporary tooth is provided.

3
Healing Period (2–4 months)

The implant fuses naturally with your jawbone through a process called osseointegration. During this time, you live normally with your temporary restoration.

4
Crown Placement — Your Final Smile (Day ~90–120)

A custom-made porcelain crown that perfectly matches your natural teeth is attached to the implant. The result is completely indistinguishable from a natural tooth.

Common Questions About Implants

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia — you will feel pressure but no pain during placement. Most patients are surprised at how comfortable it is. Post-procedure soreness (1–3 days) is manageable with standard pain medication. It is far less uncomfortable than most people anticipate.
With proper care — normal brushing, flossing, and regular dental check-ups — implants can last a lifetime. The titanium post is designed to be permanent. The crown on top may need replacement after 15–20 years due to normal wear, but the implant itself is built to last forever.
Most adults with good general health and adequate jawbone density are candidates for implants. Conditions like uncontrolled diabetes or active gum disease need to be managed first — but don't automatically disqualify you. The best way to know is to come in for a free consultation and scan, after which our specialist can give you a definitive answer.
